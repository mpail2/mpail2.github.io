You're learning how to play baseball by watching a demonstration.

Without privileged supervision like rewards and actions, you are necessarily Learning from Observation (LfO).

LfO is ideal for robot learning too, since observations are passively abundant. But, today's LfO methods still depend on large amounts of manual data and supervision.

Inverse RL drastically reduces manual supervision by learning instead from interaction and self-supervision, like practicing.

In IRL, the learner must infer the demonstrator's intent via the reward by comparing observations of them to their own.

Then RL optimizes that reward into a policy, whose new interactions refine the reward again.

But running RL to convergence, over and over, for many different rewards is impractically expensive.

Adversarial Imitation Learning updates the value and policy just once or twice per reward step.

But its policies are often brittle, generalizing poorly on real robots.

MPAIL interacts using a more robust planner, instead of a policy, by introducing a dynamics model.

But it still needs a prior dynamics model and simulation, since interaction is too inefficient.

We introduce MPAIL2 for real-world learning from observation while minimizing manual effort, like simulation and modeling.

1/3: It learns its dynamics online, in a shared latent space self-supervised by the encoder and dynamics models only.

2/3: Reward and value are learned off-policy, adding a policy network for value optimization.

3/3: A multi-step policy with a TD‑λ objective blends model-based and model-free returns.

The result is real-world LfO with no simulation, no pre-training, no rewards, and no action labels, all learned from scratch.
